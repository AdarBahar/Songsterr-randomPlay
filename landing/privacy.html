Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 17, 2026

Summary

Random Favorite Picker for Songsterr does not collect, sell, or transmit any personal information. There are no analytics, trackers, or third-party services. The extension runs entirely in your browser and only interacts with Songsterr while you use it.

What the extension stores

Your preferences are saved with Chrome's built-in storage and, if you use Chrome Sync, synced across your own devices. Nothing is sent to us or anyone else. These settings are:

  • Your preferred keyboard shortcut key
  • Your preferred instrument (Default, Guitar, Bass, or Drums)
  • Your randomization settings (mode and the newness / least-played weights)
  • Debug mode (enabled or disabled)

The extension also keeps a short, in-memory list of the last few songs you played (to avoid repeats). This is never saved to disk and is cleared when the page is closed.

How your favorites are used

To pick a random song, the extension reads your favorites list from Songsterr's own API (/api/favorites) using your existing Songsterr login, from within your browser. This data is used only to choose a song to open — it is never stored long-term, copied, or transmitted to any third party.

Permissions

The extension requests minimal permissions:

  • storage — to save the preferences listed above.

Its content script runs only on songsterr.com pages, where it adds the Random button and reads your favorites. No host permissions for other sites are requested.

Third-party services

None. The extension does not use analytics, advertising, or any external services. Fonts and assets are bundled with the extension.
